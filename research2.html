Alien & Independence Day

Alien Synopsis

In the film, space travel has gone commercial. The crew of a cargo ship land on the moon to investigate a distress call. They are unable to find anything and return to their ship. To their horror, they discover an alien has boarded their ship and is hunting them down. Some crew members manage to make it off the ship in an escape pod, however the alien also got onto the pod. The main character, Ripley, fights the alien, eventually blasting it into outer space.









Independence Day Synopsis

Independence Day takes place over the span of 3 days, July 2nd through the 4th, 1996. On July 2nd an Alien ship arrives in Earth’s atmosphere. The aliens target major cities and destroy them using lasers, killing millions of civilians. On July 3rd, the world starts to fight back against the aliens, but the alien ships have shields and attacks by humans are unsuccessful. On July 4th, the main characters of the film plant a virus on the mothership to disable the shields, and defeat the aliens.









Summary

In Alien, extraterrestrial life is portrayed in a malicious way. The film is very gruesome as the alien kills many of the crew members. In one particular scene, an alien comes out of a man’s stomach, ripping him in half. On top of violence, the lighting and music in the entire movie give it an eerie feeling, similar to a traditional horror film. Independence Day, while violent, is much less gruesome. It is also an action/thriller film, so it does not have the same horror aspects as Alien. While these two films have massive differences, the commonality between them is aliens are trying to kill humans. In whatever genre, humans being killed by aliens portrays them as a threat. These two works portray aliens as something to fear, not something to excitedly await.
